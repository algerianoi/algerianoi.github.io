Training

The key to preparing for Olympiads is relentless practice. Focus on theory alongside practical exercises. Here are some recommended resources to aid in both aspects.

For Beginners

This part is meant for people with no background in programming or people who don't know C++ :

  • A collection of written tutorials to learn C++ : w3schools C++ Tutorial
  • The EOI (Egyptian Olympiad in Informatics) C++ Course (Arabic)
  • Introduction to Algorithms, Published by the MIT Press.
  • The France-IOI training platform (French)

For Intermediate Contestants

These resources are focused on competitive programming and problem-solving techniques :

  • Competitive programmer's handbook, by Antti Laaksonen
  • Dynamic Programming for Programming Contests, by Arpan Banerjee
  • A complete guide for OI contests which is the USACO Guide
  • Competitive Programming 4 (Book 1), By Steven Halim

Training Platforms

These online judges have thousands of problems to help you train for diverse programming contests :

  • https://codeforces.com the most famous platform for competitive programming in general, it holds weekly contests in addition to its large problem set.
  • https://oj.uz and https://dmoj.ca : to access the past problems of IOI, CEOI, EGOI and other Olympiad in Informatics past papers.
  • https://cses.fi : one of the best problemsets that covers all the IOI topics.
  • https://mendo.mk : to access the past JBOI (Junior Balkan Olympiad in Informatics) tasks.
  • https://ioinformatics.org : the International Olympiad in Informatics website.
